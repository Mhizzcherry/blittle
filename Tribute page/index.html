Geroge Bernard Shaw

1856-1950

George Bernard Shaw (26 July 1856 to 2 November 1950), was famous for his role in revolutionizing comedic drama. He was also a literary critic and a prominent British socialist. Shaw's most financially successful work, Pygmalion, was adapted into the popular broadway musical My Fair Lady. He won the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1925.

A life spent making mistakes is not only more honorable, but more useful than a life spent doing nothing. George Bernard Shaw





The Following list is a timeline of Bernard Shaw's Life

1856-Born in Dublin,Ireland

1876-Moved to London to earn a living as a Literary/music Critic

1879-Publishes first novel titiled 'immaturity'

1884-Becomes a member of the Fabian Society

1892-Publishes First play- Widower's Houses.

1895-Becomes a theater critic reputation gradually expanded with additional publications

1898-Marriage to Charlotte Payne-Townshend of the Fabian Society.

1906-Moves to Ayot St.Lawrence in Hertfordshire.

1913-First Production of Pygmalion in a German translation

1916-Visits the WarZone in France

1925-Awarded the Nobel Prize for Literature.

1931-Tours Russia,the US and the Antipodes

1943-First film rendition of Pygmalion

1943-Death of his Wife

1950-Write Final Play,"Why She Would Not"

1950-Suffered Trauma and Kidney Failure,died at the age of 94
